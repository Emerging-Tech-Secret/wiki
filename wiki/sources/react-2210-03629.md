# ReAct: Synergizing Reasoning and Acting in Language Models

**Source:** Shunyu Yao, Jeffrey Zhao, Dian Yu, Nan Du, Izhak Shafran, Karthik Narasimhan, Yuan Cao.
*ReAct: Synergizing Reasoning and Acting in Language Models.* arXiv:2210.03629 [ref_1].
**Trust tier:** A (arXiv). **Snapshot:** `raw/arxiv_2210_03629.md`.

## What it says
- Reasoning (e.g. chain-of-thought) and acting (e.g. action-plan generation) had mostly been studied
  separately; ReAct combines them by generating reasoning traces and task-specific actions in an
  **interleaved** manner [ref_1].
- **Reasoning traces** help induce, track, and update action plans and handle exceptions; **actions**
  interface with external sources (knowledge bases, environments) to gather information [ref_1].
- On **HotpotQA** and **Fever**, ReAct queries a simple Wikipedia API and reduces hallucination and
  error propagation seen in chain-of-thought-only reasoning [ref_1].
- On **ALFWorld** and **WebShop**, ReAct beats imitation- and RL baselines by an **absolute success
  rate of 34% and 10%** respectively, with only one or two in-context examples [ref_1].
